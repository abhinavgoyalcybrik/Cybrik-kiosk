Prepare your child for university in Australia
Studying in Australia is an exciting opportunity for your child and an important decision for you. Australia is safe, welcoming and diverse. Your child will enjoy vibrant cities, beautiful nature and a supportive student community. At Deakin, we guide families on tuition, applications and living arrangements to help your child succeed. We can also direct you to accurate and helpful Australian Government visa information.
Reduce degree fees with scholarships
Tuition fees vary by degree. Deakin offers international student scholarships, including merit-based and country-specific awards. Some cover part of tuition, others up to 100%. We help you and your child check eligibility, apply and plan study costs.
Plan for living expenses in Australia
Living costs differ by city and campus. On-campus living includes essentials like internet and transport. We help your child plan for groceries, utilities and other expenses. Financial counselling is available to support your child’s wellbeing.
How to meet degree requirements
Students must meet academic, English language and visa requirements. If your child does not meet all requirements, Deakin offers supportive pathways. We guide families through information relating to work rights, study breaks and enrolment rules.
Get help with applications
Students can apply directly or with a Deakin international authorised agent. Agents help with entry requirements and can apply on your child’s behalf. Our team makes the application process clear and simple for both you and your child.
Deakin strives to make international students feel comfortable and at home in Australia by providing a welcoming and supportive environment, offering orientation programs, support services and a friendly community.

Why Deakin is one of the best universities in Australia
Choosing a university is about more than academics. Your child also needs a safe, inclusive and multicultural environment to grow and succeed. Deakin, with students from over 130 countries, offers world-class teaching, industry learning opportunities, excellent facilities and a vibrant campus community.
Safe and supportive accommodation
Deakin’s award-winning student accommodation offers 24/7 support, furnished rooms and inclusive utilities. Students can live on campus or nearby in secure, welcoming communities – Melbourne Burwood with city access and parklands, Geelong by the coast or Warrnambool with a supportive community.
Finding friends and support
With more than 120 clubs, societies and events – many designed for international students by international students – Deakin makes it easy for your child to connect and feel supported. They can make friends, explore new interests and access wellbeing and counselling services to help them feel at home.
World-class learning and facilities
Deakin’s modern campuses offer engineering labs, clinical simulation centres, digital media studios, industry-standard equipment and more. These facilities provide your child with practical experience and the confidence to succeed in their future career.
Part-time work opportunities for your child
In Australia, international students can work part time during their studies. This allows your child to earn an income, gain valuable work experience and start building professional networks. At Deakin, we guide students on employment rights, job search support and post-study work opportunities.

How Deakin University supports your child
From preparing to study in Australia to graduation, Deakin supports your child throughout university. As the #1 university in Melbourne, Australia for student support, we provide services that help international students feel safe, healthy and ready to succeed. Here is how Deakin supports your child to study overseas at university:
Arrival and transition support Your child receives help with airport pick-up, orientation and settling into life in Australia.
Campus safety 24/7 security and safety measures ensure your child is protected while at university.
Health and wellbeing care Your child has access to medical services, counselling, gyms, wellbeing programs and fitness facilities.
Community and connection Your child can join clubs, societies and cultural groups to make friends and feel at home.
