Academic entry requirements for coursework degrees
Before you apply, it is important to check the academic entry requirements for your chosen degree at Deakin. These entry requirements vary depending on your degree, previous studies and whether you are applying for undergraduate or postgraduate study.
For complete information on entry requirements, check the details listed on each degree page. Our international course guides provide a clear summary of what you need to apply, helping you compare degrees and plan your next steps.

Undergraduate degree
Each undergraduate degree has its own minimum entry requirements. These may vary depending on the Deakin campus where you plan to study. You can find specific details on each course page under Entry requirements – select your local qualification under Find the entry requirement for your country to discover the required secondary school qualification score for entry into your chosen Deakin degree.
Postgraduate degree
Entry requirements for postgraduate study vary by degree and study area. You can find specific details on each degree page. General admission requirements are outlined below. You can download the international postgraduate course guide (PDF, 3.8MB) for full details.
Graduate certificate or diploma
To study a graduate certificate or diploma, you must have a three-year undergraduate degree or an equivalent qualification from an approved institution. Qualifications gained through examination or relevant professional experience may also be considered.
Masters degree by coursework
To study a masters by coursework, you must have completed at least a three-year undergraduate degree or an equivalent qualification. The degree must be of an acceptable standard from an approved university or other educational institution.
Research degree entry requirements
To apply for a research degree in Australia, you must demonstrate your research ability and meet Deakin’s minimum entry requirements.
Masters degree by research
To study a masters degree by research, you must have completed a bachelor degree with honours (second class or above) from a recognised tertiary institution. Your discipline must align with an area where Deakin can provide the required supervisory expertise.
Doctor of Philosophy (PhD)
To study a PhD, you must have completed a bachelor degree with honours (second class or above) or a masters by research from a recognised tertiary institution. Your discipline must align with an area where Deakin can provide the required supervisory expertise.
Can’t find your qualification or need entry support?
If your studies or achievements are not listed, Deakin can still assess your application. We consider your academic record, work experience and other accomplishments. Our team of advisers can help you understand your options and guide you through meeting the requirements for your chosen degree.
Visa requirements
Most international students in Australia need a Student Visa (Subclass 500). Before applying, check your eligibility and ensure you meet all visa requirements. Our staff and authorised agents can help you understand visa processing, avoid delays or mistakes and support you throughout your application. For official information on visa types and conditions, visit the Department of Home Affairs website.
English language requirements
To study at Deakin University, you need to be able to read, write, speak and understand English. Each degree has specific English proficiency requirements. Our team can guide you on the level you need and how to meet it, so you are confident and prepared for your studies.
Pathways to meet entry requirements
If you do not meet the academic or English language requirements for your chosen Deakin degree, there are still ways to study with us. Pathways, including on-campus programs, TAFE qualifications and international partnerships, can help you meet entry requirements and prepare for success at Deakin. You can also explore scholarships to support your studies along the way.
Additional entry requirement considerations
Most students only need to meet the standard academic, English language and visa requirements. However, some students may need to provide extra information depending on their age or study background.
Are you under 18 years old?
If you are under 18 years old when you apply, you will need to show that you have appropriate welfare arrangements in place before a student visa can be granted. Find out how Deakin supports students under 18.
Completed an Australian Year 12?
If you have completed an Australian Year 12 in Australia or overseas, you should apply to Deakin through both the Victorian Tertiary Admissions Centre (VTAC) and StudyLink Connect. This allows us to make you a conditional offer before you receive your ATAR score.
Contact us
Our friendly advisers can answer your questions about entry requirements, study options and the application process. They can provide personalised guidance to help you meet the requirements for your chosen course.
