Deakin University English language requirements
To enrol in a Deakin degree, you will need to be able to read, speak, write and listen in English at a level that allows you to fully engage in your coursework. This level of English also enables you to successfully live and work in Australia while you study.
What are the minimum requirements?
English language proficiency requirements are aligned to the International English Language Testing System (IELTS) and Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR). Full details of the minimum criteria and how to meet them can be found in the Deakin Policy Library.
To meet these requirements, you will need to provide evidence of one of the following:
English language test results
You will need to obtain the required scores on a recognised English language test, such as IELTS, Pearson or equivalent. The minimum level for undergraduate degrees is 6.0 IELTS (CEFR B2). For postgraduate coursework degrees, the minimum is IELTS 6.5 (CEFR C1).
Please note that these are the minimum for admission to Deakin. Some courses will have higher requirements. The specific requirements for each course are available in the Entry requirements section of the course page.
Previous study in English
Recent completion of high school (secondary) or previous university studies taught in English at an approved provider can meet the requirements.
Living in an English-speaking country
If you have lived and worked in an English-speaking country for a minimum of five years, you may meet Deakin's minimum English requirements. This is evaluated on a case-by-case basis.

What if I don’t meet the English language requirements?
The Deakin University English Language Institute (DUELI) offers a wide range of programs that will help develop your English reading, writing, listening and speaking skills. Successful completion of DUELI’s English for Academic Purposes can provide you with the minimum English language requirements for most Deakin degrees.
Along with on-campus and online course work, DUELI facilitates informal, independent learning. Students can participate in extracurricular activities, such as discussion groups and watching movies in English.
Apply through an education agent
We recommend applying through one of our authorised agents around the world. They are trained by Deakin to give accurate advice about our entry requirements. They can also advise on English language tests available to you.
