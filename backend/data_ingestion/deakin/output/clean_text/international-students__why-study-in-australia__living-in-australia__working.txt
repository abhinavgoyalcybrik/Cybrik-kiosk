Your student work visa
From 1 July 2023, you can work up to 48 hours per fortnight during trimesters on your student visa. During trimesters when you are not studying and breaks between trimesters, you can work as much as you want. Make sure to balance your study and work commitments by:
- maintaining your course enrolment
- ensuring satisfactory course attendance
- ensuring satisfactory course progress.
Work experience
Professional work experience while you study can help you prepare for your future career. At Deakin, we have an emphasis on practical, real-world learning. Many of our degrees include work experience, known as work integrated learning (WIL), as a requirement or elective unit.

Your rights at work
Workers in Australia, including international students, are protected by workers’ rights. You have the right to formal pay slips, safe conditions and minimum pay. Book an appointment with Deakin Student Legal Service if you have any concerns about your working conditions in Australia.

Working after your degree
You might be able to stay in Australia and work with a post-study work visa after you graduate. Typically, a post-study work visa allows you to stay and work between two and four years, depending on your qualification.
You can apply for this visa if you have studied for a minimum of two years and completed an eligible qualification from an Australian educational institution. Eligible qualifications include:
- bachelor degree
- bachelor degree with honours
- masters by coursework degree
- masters (extended) degree
- masters by research degree
- doctoral degree.
Geelong and Warrnambool are designated regional areas. This means that graduates from Deakin campuses in those cities may be eligible for an extended temporary post-study work visa.
